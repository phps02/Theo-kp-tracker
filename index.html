Sep 25, Dec 19, and Mar 17 were geomagnetically quiet — those clusters are driven by other triggers. Maximum achievable sensitivity from Kp alone is 57%. Use alongside barometric pressure and humidity data from the main seizure monitor.
Mitigation Protocol
⚠ Moderate · Kp ≥ 4.0 × 2 periods
Heightened Awareness
  • No visitors or new people in the home
  • Maintain strict feeding and medication schedule
  • Quiet environment — minimize arousal events
  • Extended grounding (grass/soil, 20–30 min)
  • Verify evening magnesium glycinate dose
  • Log current Kp in seizure diary
🔴 High · Kp ≥ 5.0
Full Pre-Seizure Protocol
  • All Moderate actions plus:
  • Melatonin 1.5–3 mg (immediate release) that evening
  • No grooming, vet visits, or boarding
  • No high-arousal play or exercise
  • Rescue medication confirmed accessible
  • Neurologist contact confirmed available

Mechanism · Why geomagnetic storms affect seizure threshold

Geomagnetic storms suppress nocturnal melatonin via cryptochrome-mediated HPA axis activation. Suppressed melatonin lowers the threshold for hippocampal and amygdala seizure discharge propagation. Mitigation targets this pathway directly: melatonin supplementation restores suppressed levels; magnesium glycinate (NMDA antagonist) blocks geomagnetically-induced calcium flux; stress minimization prevents HPA co-activation. Source: Persinger et al. (Int J Biometeorology) · Kp data: NOAA SWPC.
